About

CS student who ships
and thinks in systems

Hi — I'm Femi, a Computer Science student at Georgia State University graduating December 2026. I started in CS because I liked building things, and I still do. But through internships, leadership roles, and real project work, I've developed an equal appreciation for the business side of technology — understanding what problems are worth solving, how data informs decisions, and how technical solutions create measurable value for organizations.

At PwC x Extern, I applied Python-based statistical analysis to a real nonprofit client's operational data and delivered findings in an executive-ready presentation. At Green Light Drivers Ed, I built automation tools and dashboards that improved data accuracy by 25% across 100+ weekly sessions. As Secretary of GSU's NSBE, I run documentation, communication workflows, and data management for a 50+ member chapter.

I'm looking for roles where I can keep building — software, systems, analytics pipelines — while also thinking about how the technology connects to outcomes. Whether that's a software engineering role, a technology analyst position, or a technology consulting program, I want to be in environments that move fast and take both technical depth and business impact seriously.
