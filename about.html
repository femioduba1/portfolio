My Story

Hi — I'm Femi, a Computer Science student at Georgia State University graduating December 2026. I started in CS because I wanted to build things, and I still do. But the more I've experienced through internships, leadership roles, and real project work, the more I've grown to appreciate the business side of technology — understanding what problems are actually worth solving, how data informs better decisions, and how technical solutions create measurable impact for organizations.

At PwC x Extern, I applied Python-based statistical analysis to a real nonprofit client's operational data and packaged findings into an executive-ready brief, visualizations, and a stakeholder presentation. At Green Light Drivers Ed, I built automation tools and dashboards that improved data accuracy by 25% across 100+ weekly sessions. As Secretary of GSU's NSBE, I manage documentation, communication, and data workflows for a 50+ member chapter.

I'm looking for roles where I can keep building — software, analytics pipelines, data systems — while also thinking about how the technology connects to outcomes. Whether that's a software engineering role, a technology analyst position, or a consulting program, I want to be in environments that move fast and take both technical depth and business impact seriously.

Outside of tech, I'm a passionate basketball fan. That's not just a hobby line — it's why I built CourtIQ, a solo full-stack analytics platform, just to dig into player data the way I wished broadcasts would.
